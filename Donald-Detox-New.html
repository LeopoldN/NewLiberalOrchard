The Employment Myth

Before we talk about Unemployment we need to lay down some facts for people who don't know much about these figures and how they're calculated. The U-3 unemployment rate is the official rate that you hear reported by the White House and News Agencies, and it gets flack by both sides whenever it fits their narrative. The main problem people have with it is that it shows the ratio between those employed and those who are included in the labor force. Where labor force is the primary point of contention as it only accounts for individuals who are either employed or actively seeking work. As a result, people who have stopped looking for work due to various reasons, such as the lack of suitable job opportunities, aren't included. In general this is a reasonably effective metric and is a reliable gauge for the economy, but to counter act people whining about it in this article, we will include just the raw employment level as well as Prime Age Labor Force Participation. Labor force participation rate is simple, they take the number of employed people and divide it by the total number of working age people. This removes the fear caused by U-3. On the other hand Prime Age restricts this number of people to those aged 25-54 in order to avoid biases caused by students in Highschool and College as well as those nearing retirement or in early retirement.

Employment is Trump's biggest talking point and it's probably one of the only metrics he fares well in. When looking at the U-3 Rate he does significantly worse than Obama, although that's less because of Trump being bad and more of Obama benefiting from starting at a higher unemployment rate due to the Great Recession.

This shows when looking at prime age LFPR, as Obama's had stayed relatively the same, while Trump actually saw more people entering into the labor force while still observing U-3 decrease. What I would like to focus on is total employment.

Under Obama employment rose 4.8% adding 7 million news jobs before Trump became president.
