Why did we create an average?

It seems as though every year we get pollsters and models telling us they have updated their data, weights, and such only for them to be off consistently. Although we believe this to be normal, human beings are inherently irrational especially when being asked questions over the phone. We here at liberal orchard think that the solution to this is not to weight certain models that we deem bad one way or another but to grab the most common/used polls and average them with equal weighting. Why? Because the solution to the irrationality of humans is not to bring in our own subjective and possibly irrational beliefs and mess with data. It's as simple as that, we will be adding a table with all of our included pollsters in the future as well as our methodology (it's pretty short).

What does this say?

All of the polls that we include are national likely voters and are closer to telling us what the popular vote would look like more than what the probability of the election will be. We are working on a model that would give us our own estimate of who is most likely to win the election in the near future, but as we stated before this brings in our own subjective beliefs. Therefore we're trying to make one that is as untouched by subjectivity as possible, and most likely wont be finished before the election :( (2028 we're gunning for you). In the mean time you can have faith that our polling average is built to give the best possible read on the national vote.
